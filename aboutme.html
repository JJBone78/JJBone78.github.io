About Me
I'm 40 years old, have been interested in computers my entire life. Started simple, with some LOGO and BASIC when I was in Grade 6, at 12 years old, as part of mandatory class work. I learned a little more in high school, took Comp Sci 11 when I was in Grade 10, and finished Comp Sci 12 in Grade 12 learning Turbo Pascal. Developed my first game as a class project, and presented it to the school Principal. And that is where I stopped. Big mistake. I've done "jobs". Not programming jobs...just regular jobs. Jobs I pretty much always hated and got bored of quickly. McDonalds. Tim Horton's. Temporary labour jobs. Office jobs. Call center jobs. But nothing ever stuck until recently, I actually held down a job for 2 years. Maybe I grew up? But as expected...eventually, i got bored, as well as sick, because it wasn't the healthiest environment. For the first time...I felt satisfaction, I enjoyed what I did, enjoyed the corporation, the people I worked with. But i still struggled to maintain my interest after the 1st year, and it showed. My attendance slipped. I still worked hard...when I was there! But eventually, they were forced to let me go. No hard feelings. I understood. I went into a depression. Spent much of my time gaming... and it hit me. At 40 years old, I'm still interested in gaming? And technology? Why don't I DO something about it? I applied for EI (still waiting), and decided...in the meantime... why don't I spend the time that I was gaming... learning? So... here I am. A beginner, since all that language knowledge of over 20 years ago is obsolete. I hope however, some of the lessons about the FUNDAMENTALS of programming stuck, even if the languages themselves won't benefit me at this stage.
